How To Use PACE
Productivity Analysis and Cost Evaluation

What does PACE do?

PACE allows you to benchmark costs and productivity for individual logging businesses. Costs are calculated in dollars per ton and then re-allocated to the entire product range ($/MBF, $/cord). Productivity is expressed in tons per machine hour.

A summary report can be provided to the supplier and submitted to your organization for aggregation.

1
Benchmark Productivity

Enter an anonymous identifier, system type, business size, and region. Then list each machine along with annual machine hours from meter readings or estimates. For machines used for non-forestry purposes (e.g., excavators), enter only hours used in connection with logging.

Next, select the predominant log rule and enter annual production in each product category. Conversion factors can be adjusted to fit your area.

2
Benchmark Costs

Enter annual overhead and variable costs. Overhead can be referenced from income tax returns. If the business has additional income beyond timber harvesting, prorate costs to reflect only the logging portion. Remove all costs associated with trucking.

Enter accurate equipment fair market values — not tax depreciation. Include a wage for owner-operators even if they don't draw a regular paycheck.

3
Report Results

Once data is entered, a complete report is compiled. Send one copy to the logger and archive a second copy for aggregate reporting. Use Print / Save as PDF to create a portable copy, or tap Submit to send the report by email.

About PACE
v7 · Productivity Analysis and Cost Evaluation

Purpose

PACE aggregates anonymous logging cost and productivity data for the benefit of all participants in the forest products supply chain. Data is separated from individual entities and used to benchmark regional cost and productivity ranges.
